# Orientation Phase — six steps before counting anything

Purpose: establish SCOPE, LANGUAGE, and EQUIPMENT before a single item is
counted. Every step ends in a human verify gate.

## Step 0 — Set scope from the GC bid request

Before opening drawings, ask (or confirm from session setup): what package is
being bid? HVAC air-side only? Combined mechanical? Does anything bleed into
plumbing (gas piping, condensate, hydronics can live on either)? Scope comes
from the GC's invitation to bid and the learner's trade — it is an INPUT to
orientation, never a mid-flow discovery. If the learner cannot answer, teach
where the answer comes from (bid invite, trade boundaries), pick the sensible
default (pure air-side, M sheets only), and state the assumption explicitly.

## Step 1 — Locate the discipline's sheets

Read the sheet index (cover sheet or first page). Propose the M-sheet list
with each sheet's number, title, and likely contents. Teach discipline
prefixes (A/S/M/E/P). Verify gate: is the list COMPLETE? Watch for mechanical
details hiding on architectural pages. Small sets (two M sheets) are normal
for light residential — note where schedules likely live (often on the
general-information M sheet).

## Step 2 — Classify the sheets by type

Sort the M list: legend/notes, schedules, floor plans, details/sections,
controls. This maps where COUNTS live (plans, schedules) versus where SCOPE
lives (notes, legends). Verify gate: classification confirmed.

## Step 3 — Read the mechanical legend (decoy guard)

Capture and interpret the M-sheet symbol key. Build a symbol table:
symbol → equipment (supply diffuser, return grille, exhaust grille, duct
up/down risers, takeoff w/ balancing damper, flex duct, motor-operated damper,
thermostat, keynote flags).

**DECOY GUARD — this failure occurred in live piloting:** general sheets
(G-series) carry look-alike content — project-wide ABBREVIATIONS lists and
ARCHITECTURAL symbol legends (callouts, tags, ADA diagrams). Neither is the
mechanical legend. If an uploaded "legend" contains no HVAC glyphs (no
diffuser/grille/damper symbols), say so, identify what it actually is, and
redirect to the M sheet. Abbreviations decode WORDS; the legend decodes the
SYMBOLS you will count.

Verify gate: symbol table confirmed, AND ask the reverse question — are there
symbols on the floor plan NOT explained in the legend? A symbol with no legend
entry is a gap to resolve before counting.

## Step 4 — Mine the mechanical general notes

Notes produce SCOPE, not counts. Read them in full and extract:

- **Complete-system clauses** ("provide all components required for a complete
  and functional system whether shown or not") — this changes the bid from
  count-what's-drawn to price-a-working-system. Flag it prominently.
- **Design responsibility** — who performs load calcs (ACCA Manual J/S) and
  duct design (Manual D)? If the contractor, that is a priced line item and it
  resolves "see remarks" sizing in schedules.
- **Spec constraints that affect measurement** — e.g., maximum flex duct
  length, duct material, leakage limits, insulation R-values.
- **Install requirements with cost** — flood elevation, hurricane tie-downs,
  clearances, termination requirements, dampers/timers on exhaust.
- **Permitting responsibility.**

Each extracted item lands either in the takeoff (as a line) or the proposal
(as an inclusion/qualification). Verify gate: learner confirms the scope
reading, especially the complete-system clause and design responsibility.

## Step 5 — Confirm the equipment schedules

Read every schedule table (AHU, fans, heat pumps, etc.): mark/tag, capacity,
electrical, and — critically — the REMARKS. Remarks carry accessory scope
(thermostats, filters, backdraft dampers, speed controllers, timers, caps)
and sizing deferrals ("model by contractor per Manual J"; "tonnage must match
AHU"). A schedule row without its remarks is half-read.

Distinguish fixed-quantity units (typically one AHU, one HP) from
count-on-plan units (exhaust fans — quantity comes from the floor plan).
Nearby installation details (mounting details) are priced install scope even
though they're not countable items.

Verify gate: unit list + accessory scope confirmed. Orientation complete —
the learner now holds the right pages, the symbol decoder, the scope rules,
and the equipment list. Only now does counting begin.
